Human Development Index

The Human Development Index (HDI) is a statistical composite of life expectancy, education, and per‑capita income indicators used to rank countries into four tiers of human development. The HDI was created to emphasize that people and their capabilities — not economic growth alone — should be the ultimate criteria for assessing a country's development.

This tool uses a Linear Regression model trained on country-level indicators to predict a country's HDI score and classify it into a development tier.
